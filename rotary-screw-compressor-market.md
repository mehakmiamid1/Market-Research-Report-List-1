<p><p>The rotary-screw compressor market is divided into two primary types: oil-free and oil-injected compressors. Oil-free compressors utilize air or other cooling methods to prevent overheating, making them ideal for applications requiring clean air, such as food processing and pharmaceuticals. In contrast, oil-injected compressors use oil to lubricate and cool the internal components, enhancing efficiency and increasing performance. These are commonly used in industrial settings where oil contamination is not a concern, such as manufacturing and heavy-duty operations.</p></p>

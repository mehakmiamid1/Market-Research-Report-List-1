<p><p>The refrigerated aircraft cargo container market is divided into two main types: active containers and passive containers. Active containers are equipped with built-in refrigeration systems, allowing for precise temperature control and monitoring during transport. They require power sources, ensuring temperature stability for sensitive cargo. In contrast, passive containers rely on insulation and refrigerants to maintain temperature for a limited time, without active cooling mechanisms. Both types play crucial roles in the air transport of perishable goods, pharmaceuticals, and other temperature-sensitive items.</p></p>

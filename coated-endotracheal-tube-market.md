<p><p>The coated endotracheal tube market includes two primary types: orotracheal intubation and nasotracheal intubation. Orotracheal intubation involves inserting the tube through the mouth into the trachea, which is commonly used in emergency settings and surgeries. In contrast, nasotracheal intubation involves inserting the tube through the nose, which is beneficial for patients with oral injuries or those requiring long-term ventilation. Both techniques utilize coated tubes to reduce friction, enhance patient comfort, and minimize complications during intubation procedures.</p></p>

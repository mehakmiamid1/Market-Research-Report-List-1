<p><p>Dissecting microscopes, used for low magnification observations, come in two main types: binocular and trinocular. Binocular microscopes feature two eyepieces, providing a three-dimensional view and greater depth perception, making them ideal for tasks requiring precision, like dissection and detailed examination. Trinocular microscopes include an additional camera port, allowing for image capture and documentation while still offering binocular vision. This versatility is beneficial in educational and research settings, facilitating visual analysis and data sharing.</p></p>
